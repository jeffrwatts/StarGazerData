Caldwell 14, also known as the Double Cluster, consists of two open clusters, NGC 869 and NGC 884, located close to each other in the constellation of Perseus. Visible to the naked eye under dark skies, this pair of clusters is a spectacular sight through binoculars or a telescope. The clusters are relatively young and contain many bright, blue stars. A low magnification, around 40x to 60x, is ideal to appreciate the full beauty of the Double Cluster, as it allows both clusters to be viewed together.
