Caldwell 33, located in Draco, is a spiral galaxy also known as NGC 5985. It's part of a galaxy trio along with NGC 5982 and NGC 5981. Caldwell 33 is a spiral galaxy with a bright core and fainter spiral arms. It's best observed under dark skies with a medium to large telescope. A magnification of 100x to 150x can reveal the galaxy's core and some structure in the spiral arms. The nearby galaxies NGC 5982 (an elliptical galaxy) and NGC 5981 (a spiral galaxy) can also be observed, offering a diverse galactic view.
