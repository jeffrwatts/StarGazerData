Caldwell 34, part of the Veil Nebula, is a supernova remnant in the constellation Cygnus. This large and complex nebula, the remnant of a supernova explosion, is best viewed with a wide field of view due to its extensive size. The nebula's delicate, filamentary structure is visible in medium to large telescopes, and an O-III or UHC filter can greatly enhance its visibility. The Veil Nebula is a stunning object in astrophotography due to its intricate details and contrasts. A magnification of 50x to 100x is recommended, although lower magnifications are preferable for wider views.
