Caldwell 49, known as the Rosette Nebula, is a large, circular emission nebula in the constellation Monoceros. The nebula surrounds an open cluster, NGC 2244, whose stars have formed from the nebula's material. The Rosette Nebula is a challenge to observe visually due to its low surface brightness but is a magnificent target for astrophotography. Through telescopes, especially with UHC or O-III filters, the nebula's shape can be discerned. The nebula is best observed with a wide-field telescope at lower magnifications, around 40x to 60x, under dark skies.
