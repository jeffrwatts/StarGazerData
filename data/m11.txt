M11, or the Wild Duck Cluster, is an open cluster in Scutum, named for its resemblance to a flock of flying ducks. It's one of the richest and most compact open clusters known. Through binoculars, M11 appears as a bright, dense patch, but a telescope reveals hundreds of stars tightly packed together. A magnification of 80x to 120x is ideal for observing the cluster, allowing you to resolve individual stars and appreciate its duck-like shape.
