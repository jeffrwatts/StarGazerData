M13, the Great Globular Cluster in Hercules, is the most prominent globular cluster in the northern hemisphere. Through binoculars, it appears as a fuzzy ball, but telescopes reveal its true splendor. Small telescopes show a dense core surrounded by a halo of stars, while larger scopes, especially over 8 inches, can resolve individual stars in the cluster. A magnification of 100x to 150x is recommended for the best views, revealing intricate details and the cluster's granular structure.
