The Eagle Nebula, or M16, is a young open cluster surrounded by an emission nebula in Serpens. Known for the famous 'Pillars of Creation,' visible in large telescopes, it's a stunning sight. Small telescopes show the nebula as a hazy patch with a cluster of stars, while larger scopes reveal the nebula's complex structure. An O-III or UHC filter can enhance the nebula's details. Ideal magnification for M16 ranges from 50x to 100x, allowing for both cluster and nebula to be observed.
