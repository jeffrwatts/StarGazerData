M22, near the top of the 'teapot' in Sagittarius, is one of the brightest globular clusters visible from the northern hemisphere. It's visible to the naked eye under good conditions and presents a magnificent view in telescopes. Small telescopes show a bright, fuzzy ball, while larger scopes begin to resolve individual stars, especially around the cluster's edges. Ideal magnifications for M22 are between 100x and 150x, revealing a dense core surrounded by a sparkling halo of stars.
