M27, known as the Dumbbell Nebula, is a bright planetary nebula in the constellation Vulpecula. It's one of the first planetary nebulae discovered and is a favorite among amateur astronomers due to its distinct shape. Through a small telescope, M27 reveals its characteristic dumbbell or apple-core shape. The nebula is quite bright and can be observed with a variety of telescope sizes, but medium to large telescopes show more details, including the finer structure within the nebula. An O-III filter can enhance the visibility of the nebula's edges. A magnification of 60x to 120x is typically ideal for observing the detailed structure of the Dumbbell Nebula.
