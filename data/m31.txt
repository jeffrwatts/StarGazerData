The Andromeda Galaxy, known as M31, is one of the most famous galaxies and the nearest spiral galaxy to the Milky Way. Visible to the naked eye under dark skies, it appears as a faint, elongated cloud. Through a telescope, even at low magnification, its bright core and elongated disk are noticeable. With an apparent magnitude of 3.4, it's a prime target for backyard astronomers. Small telescopes reveal its bright core, while larger scopes, ideally over 10 inches, start to show the dust lanes and satellite galaxies M32 and M110. No filters are required to view Andromeda, but a dark site free from light pollution greatly enhances the experience. Ideal magnification for M31 is around 50x to 100x, which provides a good balance between field of view and detail.
