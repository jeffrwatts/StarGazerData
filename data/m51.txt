M51, also known as the Whirlpool Galaxy, is a classic spiral galaxy located in the constellation Canes Venatici. It's famous for its well-defined spiral arms, visible through medium to large telescopes. At a magnitude of 8.4, M51 is best observed under dark skies. Small telescopes reveal its bright core and the hint of its spiral structure, while larger scopes, particularly those over 10 inches, can show the detailed spiral arms and its companion galaxy, NGC 5195. A moderate magnification of 100x to 150x is ideal for viewing the galaxy's structure.
