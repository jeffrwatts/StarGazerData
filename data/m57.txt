M57, the Ring Nebula, is a planetary nebula in the constellation Lyra. It's a popular target for amateur astronomers due to its distinct ring-like appearance. In small to medium telescopes, M57 appears as a small, ghostly ring or doughnut. The nebula's central star, the remnant of the original star's core, is a challenge to observe and usually requires larger telescopes and high magnification. An O-III filter can enhance the visibility of the nebula's ring structure. Magnifications around 100x to 200x are recommended to observe the details of the Ring Nebula.
