M6, also known as the Butterfly Cluster, is an open cluster in the constellation Scorpius. It's a relatively young cluster, easily visible to the naked eye under dark skies as a hazy patch. Through binoculars or a small telescope, its butterfly shape becomes apparent, revealing dozens of bright stars spread across an area of the sky roughly the size of the full moon. The cluster's brightest stars form a pattern resembling a butterfly with open wings. A low to medium magnification, around 40x to 80x, is ideal for observing M6, allowing the entire cluster to fit within the field of view.
