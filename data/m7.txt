M7, known as the Ptolemy Cluster, is a prominent open star cluster in Scorpius. Visible to the naked eye as a nebulous object, binoculars reveal its true nature as a cluster of about 80 stars. Through a telescope, M7 is a splendid sight, with its many bright stars spanning a field larger than the full moon, making it best viewed at low magnifications, around 20x to 40x. The cluster's stars are scattered across a rich field of background stars, providing a beautiful contrast.
