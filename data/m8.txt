The Lagoon Nebula, or M8, is a large and bright nebula in the constellation Sagittarius, easily visible to the naked eye in dark skies. Through a small telescope, it reveals a cloud-like structure with a noticeable dark lane, known as the 'lagoon.' Larger telescopes bring out more details, including the nebula's complex gas and dust structures. An O-III or UHC filter can enhance the nebula's contrast against the sky. Ideal magnification for M8 is around 50x to 100x, but higher magnifications can reveal finer details within the nebula.
