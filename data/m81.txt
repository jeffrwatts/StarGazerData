M81, or Bode's Galaxy, is a grand design spiral galaxy in Ursa Major, and it's one of the brightest galaxies observable from Earth. Through a small telescope, M81 appears as a bright, oval-shaped core with a faint halo. Larger telescopes reveal more of its spiral structure and brighter central region. The galaxy is best observed at moderate magnifications of around 80x to 120x, which can provide a balance between field of view and detail. Dark skies are preferred for observing the fainter details of M81's spiral arms.
