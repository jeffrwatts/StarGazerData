M82, the Cigar Galaxy, is a starburst galaxy near M81 in Ursa Major, known for its bright core and irregular shape. Through small telescopes, M82 appears as a bright, elongated smudge. Larger telescopes reveal its mottled, irregular texture, a result of intense star formation and dust lanes. The galaxy is best viewed at magnifications of 100x to 150x, allowing observers to appreciate its unique structure. M82, along with M81, makes for an excellent comparative study of contrasting galaxy types in the same field of view.
